Chia-Yu Chen

HCI Researcher & Prototyper @ Taipei, Taiwan

I'm Chia-Yu, a Master's student in Innovative User Interface Lab at National Chengchi University. I received my Bachelor's degree in Business Administration, with a double-major in Digital Contents and Technologies and a minor in Computer Science.

My research mainly focuses on Human-centered Design and Haptics. I'm particularly interested in discovering how people interact with technologies, and improving the techonology to fulfill their real need, through and by design.

PinchFun

Dec 2015 – May 2016

I'm fascinated by creating technologies that can make a impact on people.

PinchFun is a cooperative fine motor training game for preschool children with developmental delay. PinchFun was developed as a game to enhance the training of fine motor skills among preschool children with developmental delays. The control system includes a leap motion sensor and a tangible object controller embodying fine motor skills training. Players control a whale in the game by simulating its different movements. Instructors and kids need to cooperate to reach the goal. Having studied and witnessed how kids were being passive in training, I was astonished at the impact our design had on those kids training. Their long-standing negative attitude toward training and instructors both changed dramatically. This transformative experience involving human-centered design and user research has shown me how building an innovative interface could make a change in the real world. Consequently, I have become passionate about doing something that can have such a meaningful impact on people and can be used to achieve so very much. Since then I have determined that all studies that involve human-computer interaction that can assist me are essential for me to perfect my skills and become a computer scientist completely adept at HCI.
